Human firewall

Phishing awareness center

A

Check the sender

Attackers often use lookalike domains, extra hyphens, misspelled brand names, and personal email accounts for official requests.

B

Pause on urgency

Messages that demand immediate payment, password resets, OTP sharing, or account verification are common phishing pressure tactics.

C

Inspect links first

Hover, copy, or scan links before opening them. Be cautious with URL shorteners and pages that ask for credentials unexpectedly.

D

Report quickly

If you clicked a suspicious link, change passwords, revoke active sessions, enable multi-factor authentication, and report it to security.

Before you click

60-second safety checklist

  • Is the domain exactly the real company domain?
  • Does the message ask for passwords, OTPs, payment, or sensitive files?
  • Is there unusual urgency, fear, reward, or threat language?
  • Can you verify the request through an official app or known contact?
